$$\mathbf{J}_c = -N_e e \mathbf{v}_e *$$ The Drude Model - The Drude Model studies the motion of electrons in material by assuming that there are only two forces on the electrons, (1) electric force from applied electric field, $\mathbf{F}_E$ (2) force arises from the collisions among the electrons $\mathbf{F}_c$ so the long range interactions between electrons and ions are neglected.

By Newton's second law of motion, $$ m \mathbf{a} = \mathbf{F}_{total} = \mathbf{F}_E + \mathbf{F}_c $$ So, $$ m_e \frac{\partial \mathbf{v}_e}{\partial t} = -e \mathbf{E} - \frac{m_e \mathbf{v}_e}{\tau_c} $$ where $\tau_c$ is the electron collision time.

We consider only oscillatory electric fields (or alternating current) to test the material so $E_{applied} \propto e^{-i \omega t}$ or $\mathbf{E} = \mathbf{E}_0 e^{-i \omega t}$, where $\omega$ is the angular frequency of the field and $\mathbf{E}_0$ is real. In this case, we also assume that $\mathbf{v}_e \propto e^{-i \omega t}$

Now we obtain $$-i \omega t m_e \mathbf{v}_e = -e \mathbf{E} - \frac{m_e \mathbf{v}_e}{\tau_c} $$ so $$ \mathbf{v}_e = -\frac{e}{m_e} \frac{\tau_c}{1-i \omega \tau_c}$$ Recall that $$\mathbf{J}_c = - N_e e \mathbf{v}_e$$ so $$\mathbf{J}_c = \frac{\sigma \mathbf{E}}{1 - i \omega \tau_c}$$ where $\sigma = \frac{N_e e^2 \tau_c}{m_e}$.

Plasmas - We shall only talk about collisionless plasmas. Based on the Drude Model, $$\mathbf{J}_c = \frac{1}{1 - i \omega \tau_c} \frac{N_e e^2 \tau_c}{m_e} \mathbf{E}$$ where $\mathbf{E}$ is the applied electric field. We consider only oscillatory electric fields (or alternating current) to test the material so $E_{applied} \propto e^{-i \omega t}$ or $\mathbf{E} = \mathbf{E}_0 e^{-i \omega t}$, where $\omega$ is the angular frequency of the field and $\mathbf{E}_0$ is real.

In a collisionless plasmas, $\omega \tau_c \gg 1$ so $$\mathbf{J}_c \approx \frac{1}{- i \omega \tau_c} \frac{N_e e^2 \tau_c}{m_e} \mathbf{E} = \frac{N_e e^2 \tau_c}{m_e \omega} i\mathbf{E}$$

Is $\mathbf{J}_c$ imaginary? No. Since $\mathbf{E} = \mathbf{E}_0 e^{-i \omega t}$, $$\mathbf{J}_c = \frac{N_e e^2 \tau_c}{m_e \omega} e^{-i(\omega t - \frac{\pi}{2})} \mathbf{E}_0$$ $E^{real}$ and $J^{real}_c$ are out of phase by $\frac{\pi}{2}$.
